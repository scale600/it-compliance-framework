# Glossary of Terms

> **Purpose**: Standardized terminology across all IT Compliance Framework documents.

---

## A

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Anonymization** | Irreversibly transforming Personal Data so the Data Subject can no longer be identified. Anonymized data is outside the scope of GDPR. |
| **Article 30 (GDPR)** | Requirement for Controllers and Processors to maintain a Record of Processing Activities (RoPA). |
| **Audit Trail** | Chronological, immutable record of system activities (who did what, when, from where). |

## B

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Biometric Data** | Physical or behavioral characteristics used for identification — fingerprints, facial recognition, voice patterns. Special Category data under GDPR; Sensitive PI under CCPA. |
| **Breach (GDPR)** | A breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data. |
| **Business Purpose (CCPA)** | Permitted use of PI: auditing, security, debugging, short-term transient use, service provision, internal research. |

## C

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **CAP (Corrective Action Plan)** | Documented plan to remediate audit findings, including owner, timeline, and verification method. |
| **CCPA/CPRA** | California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act — comprehensive privacy law for California residents. |
| **CISO** | Chief Information Security Officer — executive responsible for information security strategy and operations. |
| **Consent (GDPR)** | Freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of the Data Subject's wishes. Must be as easy to withdraw as to give. |
| **Controller (GDPR)** | Entity that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data. |
| **Cross-Border Transfer** | Transfer of Personal Data from one jurisdiction (e.g., EU) to another (e.g., US). |

## D

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Data Minimization** | Principle that only the minimum necessary Personal Data should be collected and processed for the stated purpose. |
| **Data Subject** | The identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates. |
| **DLP (Data Loss Prevention)** | Technology and processes to prevent unauthorized exfiltration of sensitive data. |
| **DMZ (Demilitarized Zone)** | Network segment that acts as a buffer between IT and OT networks, enforcing controlled communication. |
| **DPA (Data Processing Agreement)** | Contract between Controller and Processor governing the processing of Personal Data (GDPR Art. 28). |
| **DPF (Data Privacy Framework)** | EU-US Data Privacy Framework — mechanism for lawful transfer of EU personal data to certified US companies. |
| **DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment)** | Assessment of the impact of a processing activity on Data Subject privacy, required for high-risk processing (GDPR Art. 35). |
| **DPO (Data Protection Officer)** | Designated individual responsible for overseeing data protection strategy and GDPR compliance. |
| **DSAR (Data Subject Access Request)** | Request from a Data Subject to exercise their rights (access, deletion, rectification, portability, etc.). |

## E

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)** | Integrated business management software (SAP, Oracle) managing finance, supply chain, manufacturing, HR. |
| **Encryption at Rest** | Encrypting data stored on disk/database (e.g., AES-256). |
| **Encryption in Transit** | Encrypting data as it moves across networks (e.g., TLS 1.3). |

## G

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **GDPR** | General Data Protection Regulation (EU 2016/679) — comprehensive data protection law for the European Economic Area. |
| **GRC** | Governance, Risk, and Compliance — integrated approach to managing governance, enterprise risk, and regulatory compliance. |

## H

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **HRIS (Human Resources Information System)** | Software for managing employee data, payroll, benefits, and HR processes (e.g., Workday). |

## I

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **IACS** | Industrial Automation and Control Systems — IEC 62443 terminology for industrial control systems. |
| **IEC 62443** | International standard for Industrial Automation and Control Systems (IACS) security. |
| **IoT (Internet of Things)** | Network of physical devices (sensors, actuators) that collect and exchange data. In manufacturing: temperature sensors, vibration monitors, worker tracking badges. |
| **ISO 27001** | International standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS). |

## L

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Lawful Basis (GDPR)** | One of six legal grounds required for lawful processing: Consent, Contract, Legal Obligation, Vital Interests, Public Task, Legitimate Interest. |
| **Legitimate Interest (GDPR)** | Lawful basis where processing is necessary for the Controller's legitimate interests, balanced against Data Subject rights. Requires Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA). |

## M

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **MES (Manufacturing Execution System)** | Software controlling and monitoring the production process from raw materials to finished goods. |
| **MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)** | Authentication requiring two or more verification factors (password + token + biometric). |

## N

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **NIST SP 800-53** | National Institute of Standards and Technology Special Publication 800-53 — Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems. |

## O

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **OT (Operational Technology)** | Hardware and software that detects or causes changes through direct monitoring and/or control of physical devices. In manufacturing: PLCs, SCADA, MES, sensors, actuators. |

## P

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **PIA (Privacy Impact Assessment)** | See DPIA. |
| **PII / Personal Data** | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. |
| **PLC (Programmable Logic Controller)** | Industrial computer used for automation of manufacturing processes. |
| **Privacy by Design (PbD)** | Principle that privacy and data protection should be embedded into the design of systems and processes from the outset (GDPR Art. 25). |
| **Processor (GDPR)** | Entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. |
| **Pseudonymization** | Processing Personal Data so it can no longer be attributed to a specific Data Subject without additional information (kept separately). Still considered Personal Data. |

## R

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)** | Access control based on user roles and responsibilities, implementing least-privilege principle. |
| **RoPA (Record of Processing Activities)** | GDPR Article 30 requirement — documentation of all personal data processing activities. |
| **RPO (Recovery Point Objective)** | Maximum acceptable data loss measured in time (e.g., 1 hour). |
| **RTO (Recovery Time Objective)** | Maximum acceptable time to restore service after disruption (e.g., 4 hours). |

## S

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **SCC (Standard Contractual Clauses)** | EU-approved model contract clauses for lawful transfer of Personal Data to third countries (EU 2021/914). |
| **SCADA (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition)** | System for remote monitoring and control of industrial processes. |
| **SDLC (Software Development Lifecycle)** | Process for planning, creating, testing, and deploying software. **Secure SDLC** integrates security at each phase. |
| **Sensitive Personal Data (GDPR)** | Special categories: racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union membership, genetic/biometric data, health, sex life/orientation. |
| **Sensitive Personal Information (CCPA/CPRA)** | SSN, driver's license, financial account credentials, precise geolocation, racial/ethnic origin, biometric, health data. |
| **Service Provider (CCPA)** | Entity processing PI on behalf of a business under a written contract with specific prohibitions on sale, sharing, and retention. |
| **SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)** | System for real-time analysis of security alerts from network hardware and applications. |
| **SOC 2 Type II** | AICPA audit report evaluating service organization controls over a period of time (typically 6–12 months). |

## T

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **TIA (Transfer Impact Assessment)** | Assessment required under Schrems II to evaluate the legal regime of a third country before transferring personal data. |
| **TPRM (Third-Party Risk Management)** | Systematic process for identifying, assessing, and managing risks associated with third-party vendors and service providers. |

## Z

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Zero-Trust Architecture** | Security model requiring strict identity verification for every person and device attempting to access resources, regardless of network location. |
